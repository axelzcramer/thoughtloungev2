OK... SO WHAT'S A THOUGHT LOUNGE?

A thought lounge is a formatted dialogue in which 4-6 participants each bring 1 topic, which is of utmost interest to him/her and which s/he thinks needs to be talked about, to a designated place. Each participant is given 12 minutes to conduct a presentation on his/her topic and facilitate a dialogue or activity on the topic. After each topic is given its time, all topics are written up on the blackboard and 12 minutes are given to connect them all.

THOUGHT LOUNGE POLICY FOR DIALOGUE

HERE ARE THE 5 AGREEMENTS FOR CREATING A SAFE SPACE FOR DIALOGUE


1. Listen: Participants of a thought lounge respect each other by listening to the current presenter as if s/he is the most important person in the world at that moment.

2. Appear to suspend judgment/neutral body language: When a participant is talking, body language of other participants is neutral to ensure that the ideas being divulged are exactly the perspective of the speaker.

3. Harmony not at the expense of creative conflict: When participants agree/disagree with each other, it is mutually understood that they are agreeing/disagreeing with each other’s ideas, not each other’s entire selves (unless otherwise specified o.O).

4. Present/speak from the heart: Participants share personal stories in lieu of quoting "authorities" when possible. Presenters strive to relay why their topic is in their heart. There should be a clear invitation to inquiry in each presentation. The presentations are not meant to be lectures with no possibility for falsification.

5. Equity of voice: A participant (besides the presenter) mustn’t talk for more than a minute at a time to ensure all perspectives have time to be placed on the table.
